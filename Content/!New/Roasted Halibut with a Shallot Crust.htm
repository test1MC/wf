Roasted Halibut with a Shallot Crust

Recipe courtesy of Food and Wine, 1997

1½ tbsp olive oil

5 large shallots, minced

3 tbsp unsalted butter

1 cup dried bread crumbs

2 tbsp snipped chives

2 tbsp parsley, chopped

2 tsp thyme, chopped

2 tsp of lime juice

Salt and pepper

Four 6-ounce skinless halibut fillets, 1 inch thick

 

Preheat the over to 400. butter a baking sheet (or foil).

 

In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Cook the shallots until they are soft, not brown (about 5 minutes). Transfer them to a bowl.

 

In the same skillet, melt the butter and add the bread crumbs. Stir until crisp and golden brown (about 5 minutes).

 

Add the bread crumbs to the shallots, then mix in the chives, parsley, thyme, lime juice, 1/2 tsp of salt and 1/4 tsp pepper. Toss the mixture with a fork.

 

Season the halibut on both sides with salt and pepper, then place them on the buttered baking pan. Thickly spread the bread crumb mixture on top of each fillet. Roast the fillets for 18 - 20 minutes or until done.

 

Serve immediately...

 

 

 

 

 

Yields 4 servings
