Creamy Vodka and Herb Sauce

Recipe courtesy of Bon Appétit, 1994

2+ tablespoons butter

1 sweet onion, thinly sliced

2 coves garlic, minced

1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme or 1 tsp dried

1 tbsp chopped fresh tarragon or 1 tsp dried

1 tbsp chopped fresh basil or 1 tsp dried

1 cup whipping cream

1/2 cup vodka

3/4 pound spaghetti or linguine, freshly cooked

Salt and pepper to taste

3/4 cup grated Parmesan or Romano cheese

 

Start a large pot of salted water to boil (for the pasta). When the water is at a rolling boil, add pasta and cook until done. Drain well.

 

Meanwhile, melt butter in heavy large skillet over medium heat. Add sliced onion and garlic, sauté until very tender and the onion begins to color, about 10 minutes. Add herbs and sauté 2 minutes. Add Vodka and simmer for a couple more minutes. Add whipping cream and simmer until sauce thickens slightly, about 6-8 minutes.

 

Add pasta and cheese and toss until sauce coats pasta. Season to taste with salt and pepper and serve.

 

Garnish with fresh herbs.

 

Serve over a spinach pasta (spirals, or linguine).

Add a little more basil than 1 tbsp.

Use fresh herbs for a better flavor.

 

Yields: 4 First-Course or side-dish servings.
