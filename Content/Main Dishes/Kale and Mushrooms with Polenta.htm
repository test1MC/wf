Kale and Mushrooms with Polenta

Recipe courtesy of Michela (altered from epicurious.com), year 2006

Polenta

4 cups whole milk

3½ cups water

2 cups polenta (dried polenta)

½ teaspoon salt

¾ teaspoon ground black pepper

 

Kale

1¼ pounds kale, stemmed, cut into 1-inch pieces

4 ounces mushrooms (such as crimini, oyster, and stemmed shiitake), sliced

4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided

1 garlic clove, minced

½ cup vegetable broth

2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme

1 tablespoon grated lemon peel

4 tablespoons butter (melted)

⅔ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

squeeze of lemon juice

 

Cook kale in large pot of boiling salted water until tender, about 6 minutes. Drain.

 

Bring milk, water, polenta, salt, and pepper to boil in heavy large saucepan over medium heat, whisking constantly. Reduce heat to low and simmer until thick, stirring occasionally, about 20 minutes. Remove from heat.

 

In a separate skillet, add mushrooms and 2 tablespoons oil. Sauté until mushrooms are tender, about 6 minutes. Stir in kale. Add garlic and broth; simmer until broth is slightly reduced, about 6 minutes. Stir in thyme, lemon peel, and 2 tablespoons oil. Season to taste with salt, pepper and lemon juice.

 

Whisk butter and Parmesan into polenta.

 

Divide evenly among plates, top with kale mixture and garnishes.

 

Add chili flakes to kale to add a little spice.

Garnish with parmesan cheese and chopped tomatoes.

 

Use leftover Polenta to make Chipotle Polenta Cakes (page 1)

 

Makes 6 main-course servings
