Creole Jambalaya

Recipe courtesy of Michela & Frank, November 2005

½ cup olive oil

3 cups sweet onions, diced

2 tbsp shallots, minced

1 large red bell pepper, diced

½ cup celery, diced

½ cup carrot, diced

1 medium yellow squash, chopped

1 medium green squash, chopped

1 tbsp garlic, minced

½ cup mushrooms, chopped

3 cups vine ripened tomatoes, chopped

3 tsp salt

½ tsp black pepper

2 tsp cayenne

3 bay leaves

3 cups long grain rice

1 tbs fresh thyme

6 cups vegetable broth

4 meatless sausages (Boca Italian), thawed and cut into rounds

12 oz chicken tenders (Quarn Chicken Tenders), thawed and cut into bite sized pieces

1 cup chopped green onions

 

Heat the oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Add the onions, shallots, bell pepper, celery, carrot, squash, and garlic and saute until tender about 3 to 5 minutes. Add the tomatoes and season with salt, pepper and cayenne. Add the bay leaves. Add the rice and stir for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the broth, thyme and mushrooms, stir and cover. Cook for 30 to 35 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed. Do not stir during this cooking time.

 

Remove from heat and add the "meat" let stand for 2-3 minutes. Add the green onions and mix.

 

Serve with Granny's Cornbread and sour cream.

 

We made this dish for Thanksgiving 2005 to uphold our "non-traditional" Thanksgiving meal trend. We've been wanting to replace the Jambalaya recipe from our other cookbook with a meatless recipe for sometime.

The "Chicken" and "Sausage" are optional but give it more of a meal texture and flavor, as opposed to just serving it with vegetables.

 

Yields 8 - 10 Servings
