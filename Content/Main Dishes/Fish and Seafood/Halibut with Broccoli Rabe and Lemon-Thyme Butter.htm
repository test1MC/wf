Halibut with Broccoli Rabe and Lemon-Thyme Butter

Recipe courtesy of Frank, Michela and Doug, 2008

Butter:

½ cup (1 stick) butter at room temperature

1 tbsp fresh lemon juice

1 tbsp fresh chives, chopped

1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped

 

Fish:

2 six ounce Halibut steaks about 1½ inch thick

salt

freshly ground pepper

5 - 6 tbsp olive oil

 

Broccoli Rabe (rapini):

2 - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

3 - 4 large garlic cloves, peeled and thinly sliced

1 - 1½ tsp red pepper flakes

1 bushel of broccoli rabe, roughly cut

 

1 cup jasmine rice

Lemon slices

 

Butter:

Combine all ingredients into a small bowl and mix thoroughly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

 

Rice:

Place one cup of rice in 1¾ with ½ tbsp butter. Cover and heat until it boils, reduce heat to low and cook until done. Remove from heat.

 

Broccoli Rabe:

Cook broccoli rabe in an a large pot of salted water for 2 minutes, until crisp tender. Rinse under very cold water and drain well. Set aside.

Heat oil in a pan over low heat. Add garlic and simmer until garlic is very tender, about 10 minutes (do not brown). Add the broccoli rabe and pepper flakes to the pan. Heat until warmed through. Salt and pepper to taste.

 

Fish:

Pour oil into a heavy skillet and heat until hot. Salt and pepper one side of the halibut and place that side down in the skillet. Cook for 3 - 4 minutes then salt and pepper the other side and flip the halibut over. Add a good dollop of butter between the fish and cook for 3 - 4 more minutes, until done.

 

If you do not have enough broccoli rabe, fresh spinach can be used to' fill it out'. Add the spinach when you add the broccoli rabe to the pan with the garlic.

 

 

Plating:

Melt the remaining butter in a small bowl to drizzle over fish if desired.

 

Place a small pile rice in the middle of a heated plate and spread it so that it's thin. Place a pile of broccoli rabe on the rice and spread out a bit to for a 'bed'. Place the halibut on top of the broccoli rabe and garnish with uncut chives and lemon slices.
