Ebony and Ivory Chocolate Truffles

Recipe courtesy of Death by Chocolate - The Last Word on a Consuming Passion, 1992

7½ oz white chocolate, chopped into ¼ inch pieces

8 oz semisweet chocolate, chopped into ¼ inch pieces

¾ cup heavy cream

 

Coatings:

Unsweetened cocoa

Powdered sugar

Dark chocolate cookie crumbs

Finely chopped nuts (macadamia, pecan, walnuts)

Shredded coconut

Chocolate ganache

 

Fillings:

You can place a number of nuts in the center of the chocolate and "roll" the chocolate around it.

  • Hazelnut

  • Pistachio

  • Macadamia

 

Equipment:

Cutting board

Cooks knife

Measuring cups and spoons

2 stainless steel bowels

1½ quart saucepan

Two whisks

Baking sheet

Parchment paper

 

Place the white and dark chocolates into separate stainless steel bowels.  Heat the cream in a 1½ quart saucepan over medium heat.  Bring to a boil.  Pour ¼ cup of boiling cream over the white chocolate and the remaining ½ cup over the dark chocolate and allow to stand for 4 to 5 minutes.  Stir each with a separate whisk until smooth, and allow to cool for 1 hour at room temperature.  Refrigerate the two ganaches for 15 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes.

 

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.  Portion 36 heaping tsps of the dark chocolate into separate mounds onto the parchment paper.  Top each tsp of dark chocolate with a level tsp of white chocolate.

 

To fashion the truffles, roll each portion of the chocolate in your palms in a gentle circular motion, just using enough pressure to form smooth rounds.

 

Roll rounds of chocolate in cocoa or other coatings.  Coatings can be sifted and reused for other projects.

 

If the heat of the cream is not sufficient to thoroughly melt the chocolate, or if the chocolate does not completely dissolve while stirring, then place the bowl of chocolate over very hot water (not simmering or boiling) and continue to stir into a smooth texture.

Frequently wipe hands when rolling the truffles, you may want to dust your hands with cocoa powder.

 

Truffles can be refrigerated for several days before serving.  Before serving, bring the truffles to room temperature for 15 to 20 minutes.  If the truffles absorb the cocoa powder during refrigeration, simply roll them in more powder. 

 

Yields:  about 30 truffles
