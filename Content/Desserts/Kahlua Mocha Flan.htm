Kahlua Mocha Flan

Recipe Courtesy of Michela, 2000

½ cup water

2 cups sugar

½ oz. grated unsweetened chocolate

¼ cup hot water

6 eggs

2½ cups evaporated, whole milk or cream

1 tbsp. vanilla extract

⅛ tsp. salt

½ cup of Kahlua

Whipped cream

Shaved or grated sweetened chocolate

 

In a heavy saucepan add ½ cup water and 1 cup sugar.  Stir over medium heat stirring constantly, for 3 to 4 minutes or until dissolved. Increase heat to medium-high; boil, without stirring, for about 15 minutes or until caramel colored. Quickly pour into 8 individual four-ounce custard cups (if syrup hardens, soften over low heat), evenly coating bottoms.  Set aside.

 

In a bowl dissolve chocolate in ¼ cup hot milk/cream and set aside.  In blender or mixing bowl, combine eggs, milk or cream, remaining cup of sugar, vanilla and salt.  Process for several seconds or whisk by hand until well blended.  Add dissolved chocolate mixture and ¼ cup Kahlua and stir.

 

Pour mixture into custard cups and set then into a deep-sided baking dish or pan.  Fill the outer container with water until it reaches 1 inch up the side of the of the custard cups.

 

In preheated 350°F oven bake 1 hour or until knife inserted into center of flan comes out fairly clean.  Remove cups from water bath and allow to cool completely.  

 

Add a small amount of lemon juice or corn syrup to the sugar and water mixture as soon as it begins to boil.  This will stop the sugar from crystallizing and ensure a nice caramel versus a pan full of sugar crystals.

 

To unmold flan, run tip of wet knife along inside edge of cup. Place serving dish up-side down over mold and quickly flip both the dish and mold. 

 

 Yields: 8 servings
