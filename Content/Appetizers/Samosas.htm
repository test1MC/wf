Samosas

Recipe courtesy of Frank, 2000

Pastry:

2 cups flour

½ tsp salt

4 tbsp oil (vegetable or canola)

4 tbsp water (add more as needed)

 

Stuffing:

4-5 medium potatoes (russet)

4 tsp oil

1 medium onion (finely chopped)

1 cup peas (shelled - frozen work ok)

1 tbsp ginger (finely grated)

1 fresh hot green chili (finely chopped)

3 tbsp cilantro (finely chopped)

3 tbsp water

1½ tsp salt

1 tsp ground coriander seeds

1 tsp garam masala

1 tsp ground cumin seeds (roasted)

¼ tsp cayenne pepper

2 tbsp lemon juice

oil for deep frying

 

Pastry:

Sift salt and flour into bowl. Mix in the oil with your fingers, until the mixture resembles coarse bread crumbs. Slowly add 4 tbsps of water (use more is needed. Gather the dough into a stiff ball. Knead the dough on a clean flat surface for about 10 minutes or until smooth. Gather it into a ball and rub it with oil (about 1/4 tsp). Place it in a plastic bag and set it aside for at least 30 minutes.

 

Stuffing:

Boil the potatoes in their skins and let them cool. Peel the potatoes and cut them into 1/4 inch dices. Heat 4 tbsp oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat. Fry onions until the edges are browned. Add peas, ginger, green chili, cilantro, and 3 tbsp water. Lower heat and cover. Simmer until peas are cooked. Stir to keep from sticking and add water if it becomes dry.

Add potatoes, salt, coriander seeds, garam masala, cumin, cayenne and lemon juice. Stir to mix. Cook and stir for about 3 - 5 minutes. Taste... You may want to add more salt, lemon juice or cayenne. Allow the mixture to cool.

 

Putting it together:

Knead dough again and divide it into eight balls of equal size. Keep the balls of dough that are not being used covered with a damp towel. Roll one of the balls out to about a 7 inch disk. Cut the disk in half. Form the half into a cone, sealing the seams with water. Stuff the cone with about 2 - 2 1/2 tbsps of the stuffing. Close the top of the cone by sticking the open edges together. Use a fork or fingers to press the two seams firmly together. Make 7 more using this method. Cover finished samosas with damp towel.

 

Fry (Note: try baked instead of fried) samosas until golden brown. Drain completely. Serve warm, hot or at room temperature.

 

Try this baked instead of fried. Use philo dough for baking. Serve with Green Chutney (see page 1).
