Chorizo and Potato Spanish Tortilla Bites

Recipe courtesy of unknown, adapted by Michela, 2010

1 bunch scallions (green onions)

2 tbs extra virgin olive oil

2 medium sweet onions, chopped

4 garlic cloves, finely chopped

Salt and pepper (original recipe calls for 1¼ tsp salt and ½ tsp of pepper but I didn't measure)

1 package Trader Joe's Brand Soyrizo

1½ lb of red potatoes - about 4 medium, peeled and chopped in ¼ dice

10 large eggs

¾ cup sour cream (lite)

5 oz of Manchego Cheese (about 2+ cups) - grated (I also mixed in Mexican blend)

 

Preheat oven to 400

Lightly oil 13 x 9 casserole

  • Finely chop green onions separating white from green parts
  • Heat oil in skillet and add onions, garlic, white part of the green onions, salt and pepper and stir occasionally until onions release juices and start to brown.
  • Add Soyrizo and brown
  • Reduce heat and add potatoes, stir and cover. Cook until potatoes are just tender.
  • Transfer to a bowl to cool
  • In separate bowl whisk eggs, sour cream, cheese and green part of green onions. Make sure to whip good to blend the sour cream completely and the egg batter is light and fluffy. Salt and Pepper
  • Pour mixture into greased casserole dish
  • Sprinkle the soyrizo mixture over top of the egg mixture making sure it is evenly distributed.
  • Bake until custard is set about 20-30 minutes depending on oven.
  • Cool to room temperature and cut into 1 1/2 -1in pieces.

 

I found that making this a day ahead, completely cooled then storing in the fridge until the next day makes them a little easier to cut and serve as a finger food appetizer. Serve at room temperature or warm in 325 oven.
