Stuffed Grape Leaves

Recipe courtesy of Frank Novak, 1998

2½ cups cooked Quinoa (cooked in vegetable broth)

1 cup sweet onion, minced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 - 2 stalks celery, minced

¼ cup sunflower seeds

2 - 3 tbs olive oil

2 tbsp fresh mint, minced

Juice from one lemon

¼ cup fresh parsley, minced

1 cup ricotta cheese

Salt and pepper

About 40 grape leaves (½ lb or 4.5 oz jar)

 

Cook quinoa and let cool a little (so you don't get burned)

 

Heat a saute pan and add oil. When hot, saute the onion, garlic, celery and sunflower seeds until the onions are soft. Salt and pepper to taste.

 

In a large bowl combine 2 1/2 cups of the cooked quinoa and all the ingredients from the saute pan. Mix in parsley, mint, 1/2 of the lemon juice and ricotta cheese. Taste and adjust accordingly with lemon juice, mint, quinoa, cheese, salt and pepper.

 

Remove grape leaves from the jar, being careful not to tear them. Rinse very well in cold water to remove the bitterness from the brine.

 

Place a leaf down flat, stem toward you, on a clean working surface. Place a heaping tbsp (more or less) of the filling near the base of the leaf (stem) and roll tightly, folding in the sides. Place on a lightly (olive) oiled cooking sheet. Lightly spray or brush the tops with olive oil. Bake 20 - 25 minutes, covered. Uncover and bake another 5 minutes if they appear too moist.

 

Let cool for 5 - 10 minutes before serving. Serve with lemon wedges and sauce.

 

Serve with Spinach & Feta Yogurt Sauce (page 1)

 

  • Quinoa can be replaced by brown rice.
  • If you use fresh grape leave, pick them around June or they will be too tough.
  • This can be served as a main dish over a bed of rice pilaf and a salad.
  • Reese grape leaves are the best that we've found.

 

Yields: approximately 40 grape leaves
