Asparagus and Fresh Dill Pastries

Recipe courtesy of Frank, 2000

½ pound philo pastry (frozen)

½ tsp canola oil

1 lb asparagus spears (thin)

½ medium red bell pepper

1 tbsp unsalted butter (melted)

1 tbsp olive oil

4 oz soft chevre

¼ cup fresh dill springs (loosely packed)

pepper (several grinds)

 

Remove philo pastry from the freezer and allow to thaw in its packaging for about 4 hours before preparing the pastries.

 

Rub a cookie sheet or baking tin with the canola oil. Rinse the asparagus and remove the tough part of the stalk. You want the asparagus to be about 5 inches in length. Cut the bell pepper in half length wise and cut it into 8 thin strips. Mix the melted butter with the olive oil in a small bowl. Have a pastry brush on hand.

 

Preheat the oven to 375°. Keep the dough that you are not working with covered at all times with a slightly damp tea towel so that it doesn't dry out. Lay out one sheet of dough, brush it very lightly with the butter/oil mixture, lay another sheet on it, brush it with the mixture. Proceed with this pattern until you have a stack of 5 sheets.

 

Starting at one of the short ends, place about a tablespoon of the soft chevre across the dough, but leave about 1 inch free on each side. Arrange one to two asparagus spears, a red bell pepper strip and several small sprigs of dill on top of the chevre. Grind a touch of pepper on it, fold over the free sides and roll up. Moisten the edges with a little water to seal. Place on the cookie sheet and prepare the 7 remaining rolls, keeping the prepared ones covered so that they do not dry out.

 

Bake for 35 minutes or until golden brown. Serve hot.

 

 

 

 

 

Yields: 8 pastries
