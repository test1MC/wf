Mexican Rice

Adapted from Leith's Latin-American Cooking by Frank, 2010

1 cups + 2 tbsp long grained rice, uncooked

3 medium tomatoes, halved

1 sweet onion, halved

2 cloves garlic, crushed and minced

4 tbsp vegetable oil

Salt and fresh ground pepper

1¾ cups of vegetable broth, hot

2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped

¾ cup frozen peas, thawed

1 green chile, seeded and finely diced

½ red bell pepper, seeded and finely diced

1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

 

Wash the rice in several changes of water and let it soak for 15 minutes. Drain it thoroughly.

Drizzle the vegetables with olive oil. Salt and pepper then and grill the until soft and slightly charred. You can use the broiler if you prefer.

Place the vegetables and garlic in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Add broth if the puree is too thick. Optional: Strain the puree.

 

Heat 2 tbsp of oil in a heavy sauce pan over medium-high heat. Add the rice and cook, stirring constantly, until the rice is lightly golden brown (about 5 minutes). add the vegetable puree and cook for 5 minutes.

 

Stir in the carrots, peas, chili, red pepper and broth. Season with salt and pepper and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and cover. Simmer until the rice is tender and the liquid absorbed (about 20 minutes).

 

Transfer to a serving dish and sprinkle with parsley.

 

 

Grill some tomatillos, sorrento or jalapeno chilies to add a little spice if desired.

 

You can add other vegetables to this dish when you add the carrots and red peppers

  • zucchini, mushrooms, corn, etc.

 

You can also sprinkle cheddar cheese over the rice about 5 minutes before it is done.

 

Yields: 4 servings
