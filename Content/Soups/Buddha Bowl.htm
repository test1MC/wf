Buddha Bowl

Recipe courtesy of Isabel's Cantina, 2007

2 garlic cloves, roughly chopped

¼ cup lemon grass bulb, roughly chopped

¼ cup fresh ginger, roughly chopped

2 tbsp peanut oil

½ medium onion, chopped

1 medium carrot, chopped

1 celery stalk, diced

one 14 ounce can of coconut milk

½ cup white miso paste

4 cups cold water

1 lb dry rice noodles

 

Place the garlic, lemon grass, ginger into a food processor and pulse until it is finely minced. Set it aside.

 

Heat the oil in a large heavy-bottomed soup pot over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 4 minutes. Add the carrot and celery and continue cooking until softened, about 4 more minutes. Add the garlic mixture and stir to combine. Cook for another 2-3 minutes and then add the coconut milk, miso paste, and 4 cups of cold water. Bring the soup to a simmer, being careful not to boil. Lower the heat and continue to simmer (not boil) until the miso paste has gently dissolved, 25 - 30 minutes.

 

Cook rice noodles as described on the box.

 

To serve, place a mound of rice noodles in the center of each bowl. Ladle broth over, garnish and serve immediately.

 

We've never made this the same way twice. Feel free to experiment with the quantities of everything.

 

Have lime wedges and sriracha hot sauce or chili paste to add to individual bowls.

Other garnishes: chopped cilantro and mint, green onions, peanuts, chile flakes.

 

Yields: 4 - 6

 

The broth can be sealed, refrigerated and stored for up to three days. reheat gently without boiling.

 

To store the rice noodles: Place them in cold water and cover. they will keep in the refrigerator for a couple of days. Only reheat the portion that you will reuse.
