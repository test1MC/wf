Butternut Squash and Roasted Garlic Bisque

Recipe Courtesy of Frank, 2000

¼ cup butter

3 cups chopped onions (sweet)

¾ cup chopped carrot

½ cup chopped celery

4 pounds butternut squash - peeled and seeded - cut into 1-inch pieces

6 cups vegetable broth

2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage

½ cup cream (Whipping)

2 Heads of garlic (halved crosswise)

1 teaspoon olive oil

 

Garlic:

Preheat oven to 350. Drizzle cut surfaces of garlic with oil. Put the halves back together, wrap tightly in foil and bake until tender. About 40 minutes. Cool garlic in foil. Squeeze garlic cloves into a bowl and mash it with a fork until smooth.

 

Soup:

Melt butter in heavy large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots and celery and saute until onions are getting soft (about 10 minutes). Add squash, broth and 2 tablespoons of sage. Bring to boil, reduced heat and simmer uncovered until squash is soft (about 25-30 minutes).

 

When the squash is soft stir in the garlic paste. Puree soup in blender until smooth. Return to pot. Bring to simmer and add 1/2 cup cream. Season to taste with salt and pepper.

 

Garnish:

Drizzle a small amount of cream onto the soup and add a whole sage leave to it (or sprinkle remaining chopped sage on it).

 

 

Yields: 12 servings
