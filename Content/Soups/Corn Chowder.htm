Corn Chowder

Recipe courtesy of Vegetarian Times, September 2011, altered by Frank

The corn and leeks are essential in this soup, feel free to substitute sweet potatoes, carrots, or other root vegetables for some or all of the potatoes. Also you can use various types of beans, yellow wax or purple instead of green beans.

 

5 large ears of sweet corn, kernels cut from the cob, reserve the cobs

2½ cups low-fat milk

3 cloves of garlic, minced

3 cloves of garlic, crushed

2 tbs. unsalted butter

3 tbs. olive oil

3+ cups sliced leeks

½ tsp. smoked paprika

⅓ cup dry sherry

12 oz. potatoes, medium diced

½ lb. green beans cut into ½ inch pieces

2 tbs. chopped fresh cilantro

Chipotle pepper flakes (optional)

Salt & pepper

Lime wedges

 

Combine corn kernels, milk and crushed garlic in a saucepan. Run the back of a knife down cobs to release milk and pulp into the saucepan, then add the cobs to the pan. Bring to a boil, stirring often, remove from heat and let steep.

 

Heat butter and 1 Tbs. oil in a Dutch oven over medium-low heat. Add leeks, cover, and cook 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add minced garlic and paprika, cook 30 seconds. Stir in sherry and cook for 30 seconds. Add 4 cups water, and remove the pot from heat.

 

Heat 1 Tbs. oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add potatoes, and saute 8 minutes or until browned; transfer to Dutch oven. Add the remaining 1 Tbs. oil to the same skillet, add green beans, and saute for 3 minutes. Transfer beans to plate.

 

Bring the mixture in the Dutch oven to a boil, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer 5 minutes. Add green beans and cook for 4 more minutes.

 

Discard the cobs from the milk mixture. Stir milk mixture and 1 Tbs. cilantro into the chowder (Dutch oven). Season with salt, pepper and chipotle pepper flakes to taste if desired.

 

Serve and sprinkle with remaining cilantro and lime wedges.

 

 

 

 

Yields: 6 servings (1⅔ cup servings)

Calories: 280
