New Mexico Onion Soup

Recipe Courtesy of Frank, 2000

6 large onions about 3½ lbs thinly sliced - sweet or yellow

7½ cups vegetable broth

1 teaspoon ground cumin

½ teaspoon ground coriander

½ teaspoon dried oregano

⅓ cup of all-purpose flour

2 tablespoons red chili powder

canola oil

salt (if needed)

 

Garnish:

6 corn tortillas

1¼ shredded cheddar/jack mixture

1/3 cup chopped cilantro

 

Soup:

In 5-6 quart pan combine onions, 1 cup of the broth , cumin, coriander and oregano. Cook over high heat, stirring occasionally, until almost all of the liquid has evaporated. Continue to cooking (and stir) until the onions are very soft and turning caramel in color.

 

Smoothly mix the remaining broth with the flour. Stir chilli powder into onion mixture and cook, stirring, for about more 1 minute, then stir in the broth mixture. Bring to a boil then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer (for about 15 minutes, uncovered, stirring often).

 

Tortillas:

Pour oil (about ¼ inch deep) into a saute pan and place over medium -medium high heat. Stack the tortillas and cut them into ¼ inch strips. Deep fry a few of the tortilla strips at a time, until golden brown. Then place them on a paper towel to drain (salt immediately).

 

Ladle soup into bowls, sprinkle with tortillas chips, cheese and cilantro.

 

 

 

 

 

Yields about six 1 cup servings
